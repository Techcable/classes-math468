        \item 
            The CDF $P(X\le k)$ of the geometric RV can be computed using the partial sum of  a geometric series:
            \[ P(X\le k)=\sum_{i=0}^k pq^{k-1}=\frac{p(1-q^{k+1})}{1-q}=1-q^{k+1} \] 
            Using the def. of conditional probability and the fact $X>n+m \implies X>m$ we get
            \begin{align*} 
                & P(X>n+m|X>m)\\
                = & \frac{P(X>n+m,X>m)}{P(X>m)} \\
                = & \frac{P(X>n+m)}{P(X>m)} \\
                = & \frac{1-P(X\le n+m)}{1-P(X\le m)} \\
                = & \frac{q^{n+m}}{q^m} \\
                = & q^n \\
                = & 1-P(X\le n) \\
                = & P(X>n)
            \end{align*}
            This proves that $X \sim \text{Geom}$ has the desired property. \qed
            \\ 
            Now assume that $Y$ is an exponential random variable with PDF $f_Y(x)=\lambda e^{-\lambda x}$. 
            The CDF of $Y$ is $P(Y\le x)=F_Y(x)=\int_0^xf_Y(t)dt=-1-e^{-\lambda x}$. Once more, using the def of conditional probability and $Y>n+m\implies Y>m$ we get
            \begin{align*}
                & P(Y>n+m|Y>m)\\
                = & \frac{P(Y>n+m,X>m)}{P(Y>m)} \\
                = & \frac{P(Y>n+m)}{P(Y>m)} \\
                = & \frac{1-P(Y\le n+m)}{1-P(Y\le m)} \\
                = & \frac{1-P(Y\le n+m)}{1-P(Y\le m)}  \\
                = & \frac{e^{-\lambda (n+m)}}{e^{-\lambda m}} \\
                = & e^{-\lambda n} \\
                = & 1-P(Y\le n)  \\
                = & P(Y>n)
            \end{align*}
            This proves the desired property for $Y\sim \text{Exp }\lambda$. \qed
